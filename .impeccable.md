## Design Context

### Users

Ecommerce operators and brand-side ecom/marketing directors at established Shopify or Shopify Plus brands — $2M+ GMV, past the scrappy stage. They've typically been burned by a slow agency or a developer who didn't understand the business side. They know exactly what they want built but can't build it themselves. Sophisticated enough to recognize quality, budget-authorized to pay for it.

**The job to be done:** Find someone who gets the business, not just the code.

**Emotional goal:** Relief, grounded in trust. Not excitement or hype — the quiet confidence of finally finding the right person. The site should feel like exhaling.

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** Grounded. Sharp. Unhurried.

- **Grounded:** Operator-side experience, not agency-speak. Real stakes, real P&L thinking.
- **Sharp:** Precise language, considered structure, no filler. Quality is obvious without announcing itself.
- **Unhurried:** Selective by design. Doesn't need every client. Confident enough to say no.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme:** Light mode. The audience browses during business hours, often on desktop, in professional contexts.

**References (quiet typographic studios):** Work & Co, Ragged Edge — restrained, typographically led, nothing shouting.

**Anti-references:** The oversaturated "we scale ecom brands 🚀" agency aesthetic. Loud gradients, generic Webflow templates, dark-mode maximalism, stock photo founders pointing at laptops. Nothing that reads as trying too hard.

**Current state assessment:** Strong bones — the type pairing and green palette are right. The main tension: cream + Instrument Serif can pull slightly toward artisan/vintage when the target feel is more precise and considered. The fix is structural crispness, not a palette overhaul.

**Direction going forward:**
- More white as the default surface; cream used as an intentional accent, not the baseline
- Forest green used with high contrast around it — reads precise and deliberate, not organic and warm
- Warmth should feel like restraint, not softness
- Structural crispness: tight spacing, clean edges, confident whitespace

### Colors

- **Forest green (#2D5F4A):** Stays. Use it with high contrast so it reads precise, not earthy.
- **Cream (#F5F0E8):** Use sparingly — accent surfaces only. Too much tips into vintage.
- **White:** The primary surface color going forward.
- **Charcoal (#1A1A1A):** Primary text. Strong, confident.
- **No colors explicitly off-limits**, but avoid anything that softens or warms the palette further.

### Design Principles

1. **Precision over warmth.** Every spacing, sizing, and color decision should feel deliberate. If it reads cozy or artisan, pull back.
2. **Trust through restraint.** The site earns credibility by not overselling. No hype words, no loud visual moments, no "we scale brands 🚀" energy.
3. **Contrast that communicates.** Forest green only appears where it needs to — CTAs, key accents. Its rarity is its power.
4. **Typography does the work.** Hierarchy, weight, and rhythm carry the page. Decorative elements are earned, not default.
5. **Accessibility as craft.** `prefers-reduced-motion` respected, WCAG AA contrast on all text, minimum 44px touch targets. Not a checklist — part of the quality bar.

### Technical Stack

- **Framework:** Astro 4.16
- **Styling:** Tailwind CSS 3.3 with custom tokens (`forest`, `forest-light`, `forest-pale`, `cream`, `charcoal`)
- **Animation:** sal.js (scroll-triggered, `once: true`, `threshold: 0.1`)
- **Interactivity:** Alpine.js (mobile menu, contact form state)
- **Fonts:** Instrument Serif (headings) + Plus Jakarta Sans (body) — both are AI-reflex choices; revisit at next type pass
- **Deployment:** Netlify
